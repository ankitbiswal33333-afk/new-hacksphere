1. Aim of Experiment

To study and plot the V-I Characteristics of a PN Junction Diode and a Zener Diode under Forward and Reverse Bias conditions, and to determine the Cut-in Voltage and Breakdown Voltage.

2. Theory

PN Junction Diode

A PN junction diode conducts current primarily in one direction (Forward Bias). When the p-type side is connected to the positive terminal and n-type to the negative, the depletion width decreases.

  • Cut-in Voltage (Knee Voltage): The minimum voltage at which the diode starts conducting significantly.
    • Silicon (Si): ~0.7 V
    • Germanium (Ge): ~0.3 V
Shockley Equation: I = Is * ( e^(V / nVt) - 1 )

Zener Diode

A Zener diode is heavily doped and designed to operate in the Reverse Breakdown region. Once the reverse voltage exceeds the Zener Voltage (Vz), the current increases sharply while voltage remains constant.

3. Procedure
  1. Select the Material (Silicon, Germanium, or Zener).
  2. Set the Temperature (Standard room temp is 27°C).
  3. Turn ON the Main Power Supply switch.
  4. Use the slider to vary the DC Voltage ($V_{in}$) from 0V to +2V (Forward) and 0V to -5V (Reverse).
  5. Click ADD READING to plot the point and log the value.
  6. Observe the Saddle Point (Knee) on the graph.
  7. Compare your experimental graph with the Theoretical Curve by checking the box.
